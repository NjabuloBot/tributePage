Here is a time line of Marcus Garvey :

Early life

  • Born into a poor Jamaican family in 1887, young Marcus Garvey left school at 14 to
    become a printer's apprentice.Fueled by Pan-Africanist ideals and inspired by travel
    and intellectual pursuits,he founded the Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA)
    in 1914 to advocate for Black unity, economic empowerment, and a return to Africa.
  • Moving the UNIA's headquarters to Harlem in 1916, Garvey ignited a movement for racial pride
    and self-determination among Black Americans, leaving a lasting legacy despite facing criticism and
    legal challenges.
  • Though controversial, Garvey's impact on Black cultural awareness and the Pan-African movement remains undeniable,
    inspiring generations of activists and leaders fighting for racial equality and African liberation.

Early Career

  • After leaving school at 14, Marcus Garvey rose through the ranks as a printer in Jamaica,
    becoming involved in labor activism and absorbing Pan-Africanist ideas.
  • Traveling to Central America and London exposed him to diverse experiences and philosophies,
    further shaping his vision for black unity and empowerment.
  • In 1914, he founded the Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA) in Jamaica, advocating
    for black self-determination, economic independence, and a return to Africa.
